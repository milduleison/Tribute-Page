Painter - Petras Kalpokas
1880 - 1945

Biography


Petras Kalpokas was a painter, teacher and active member of the community. He was born on 1880 Forestry (Birzai district). Died in 1945 In Kaunas. Artist Rimto Kalpok's father. 1895-1898 studied painting at Mintajuja (Latvia) with V. Purvytys and J. Valter. 1898-1900 studied at the Odessa School of Arts (Ukraine). Later studied painting at the Art School in Munich, A. Ažbe. Since 1907 One member of the group of artists and architects Sezession. In Munich, Petras Kalpok was considered a more moderate creative person who bases on the principles of impressionism when whose symbols characterize his paintings are secular. Art exhibitions have taken place since 1898. There was one founder of the Lithuanian Art Society (1907). Until the First World War he lived in Switzerland, Hungary, and Latvia. The wartim he spent e in Switzerland and Italy, on the Sestri Levante peninsula near Genoa, and after the war he moved to Rome. In 1920 he returned to Lithuania 1921 In Kaunas began teaching paintings from 1929-1940. He was the head of the Art School at Kaunas Art Academy in 1941-1945. Worked at the Kaunas Applied and Decorative Arts Institute. Since 1904 P. Kalpok participated in exhibitions organized by the Lithuanian Art Society in Lithuania and abroad (at the World Exposition in New York, 1939).Individual exhibitions were held in Kaunas (1928), Vilnius (1955). Peter Kalpok came from German art in Latvia and, after experiencing the influence of the art of Munich, became one of the pioneers of Lithuanian folk renaissance art, developed Impressionism, Symbolism
